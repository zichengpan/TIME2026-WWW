# **TIME 2026 Workshop – Call for Papers Summary (English)**

## **Themes & Topics**

In today’s digital landscape of privacy breaches, misinformation, and algorithmic bias, this workshop fosters cross-domain collaboration, applying successful techniques across fields. By bridging academia and industry, it aims to translate research into actionable solutions for modern web challenges.

Previous edition link: [TIME 2025@WWW 2025]. Building on the success of the 2025 edition, the next iteration of the TIME workshop will broaden its thematic scope to include new topics such as responsible AI evaluation, trustworthy multimodal analytics, and cross-domain bench-marking frameworks. It will also introduce an industry collaboration track that fosters closer interaction between academic researchers and practitioners.

### **1. Responsible and Cross-Domain Evaluation**

Topics include:

* Responsible AI evaluation (fairness, robustness, transparency, societal impact)
* Trustworthy multimodal analytics (video-text, audio-text, sensor-video, etc.)
* Cross-domain benchmarking frameworks (healthcare, social media, smart cities, security)
* Industry collaboration: case studies, deployed systems, academic–industry evaluation work

---

### **2. Survey Papers**

Comprehensive literature reviews such as:

* Video processing techniques (compression, enhancement, analytics)
* Data-centric video analytics and ML-driven video understanding

---

### **3. Evaluation-Focused Studies**

Critical evaluations of existing systems, including:

* Ethical & trustworthy AI for healthcare
* Security & privacy in clinical AI
* Video content moderation evaluation (accuracy + ethical implications)

---

### **4. Methodology Reviews**

Analysis of methods and opportunities for improvement:

* Data standards & annotation practices for AI/ML
* Robust and interpretable LLMs for healthcare
* Video analysis methods for social media content

---

### **5. Cross-Disciplinary Insights**

Interdisciplinary applications of web technologies:

* Smart city applications with AI-driven governance
* Strategies to combat online extremism, hate speech, harassment
* Human-centric video analytics (health, security, behavioral studies)

---

### **6. Emerging Challenges**

Evaluation of current issues and future recommendations:

* Misinformation/disinformation during crises
* Quality, uncertainty & trust in discourse data
* Challenges in video quality assessment
